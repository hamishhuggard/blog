Notes from Better Data Visualizations

Write titles like newspaper headlines. Rather than "A vs B" do "x has Fallen, y has Risen"

Always ask "is more annotation useful"

You can communicate uncertainty with a hand drawn/sketchy plot

Bubble-on-map plots are "proportional symbol maps"

Arc chart = unrolled chord chart

Research indicates that people read pie charts as segment lengths more than as angles

Nightingal chart

John Snow's cholera map is actually a Voronoi diagram

Right align numbers in tables to the decimal places line up

Spacing between rows and columns in tables can make it look like rows are groups or that columns are groups

Consider putting units or "%" or order of magnitude in the first row only

The alt text of an image is an alternative to seeing the image itself. For example, for blind visitors.

Dorling and Demers Cartograms

Judy Olson: "Probably one of the most interesting aspects of the non-contiguous cartogram is the that empty area between units is meaningful. If the highest-density unit is used as the anchor [in other words, if the most dense geographic unit is used to scale all the other units], then the empty areas reflect the degree of discrepancy between the density in the most crowded unit and the density in other units."

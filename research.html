Detecting Concept Drift in Medical Triage

2020, ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval

I develop an algorithm for detecting model miscalibration as a proxy for dataset shift or concept drift.

The paper is focused on biomedical applications of machine learning, as this was the original motivation and source of funding, but really it's generally applicable to deployed/online models.

This research was came out of my masters thesis. See below for more context.

Concept Drift in Medical Referrals Triage

2020, M.Sc. thesis.

My M.Sc. was funded by a scholarship form the Presicion Driven Health Partnership (PDH) and concerned figuring out when a medical ML model needs retraining.

I won the annual "best M.Sc." prize from the computer science department, and one of my examiners said it was "the best masters thesis they had seen".

There are some unpublished ideas in here which I hope to return to at some point.

Feature Importance for Biomedical Named Entity Recognition

2019 Australasian Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence

I did this research during an intership at Orion Health.

The paper surveys features which have been used in biomedical natural language processing, and investigates the value of feature engineering when taking a deep learning approach to biomedical natural language processing.

Predicting Air Quality from Low-Cost Sensor Measurements

2018 Australasian conference on data mining

In the summer between my undergraduate and honours degrees, I worked with the National Institute of Water and Atmospheric Research (NIWA) on modelling air quality using low cost sensors.

Low-cost sensors pose a number of challenges: they are unreliable, noisy, and may require ongoing calibration. This paper explores these challenges, and evaluates several approaches to spatiotemporal modelling of air quality using the sensors.
